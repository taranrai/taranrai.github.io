About

What I do

I do AI/ML engineering and data science things. My work spans computer vision, deep learning, LLMs, and applied AI/ML. I build and validate algorithms for real-world deployment, and I stay close to the cutting edge of what's happening in AI.

A lot of my research has been in computational pathology and the health domains (both animal and human), but I've also worked across social media listening with pharma companies, wearable tech and sensor-based systems through the vHive Animal Health Incubator, and I'm interested in the human-technology interaction side of AI. My experience spans niche startups through to larger multinational organisations.

I guest lecture for AI and AI Programming at the University of Surrey, introducing neural networks.

I hold a PhD in AI/ML for digital pathology (deep learning + computer vision for grading in canine sarcoma biopsy images), an MSc in Data Science from City, University of London, and a BA (Dual Hons) in Economics and Finance from Keele University.

Some of my passions

My interests are shaped by personal experiences. I choose to work on projects that genuinely matter to me.

Applying and developing AI/ML models for animal and human health. Most of my research lives here.

🌾

Protecting and providing for future generations. Farming, planting lots of trees, and nurturing the land.

💸

Investment Accessibility

Collaborating with investment managers from boutique investment banks to make investing easier to understand and more accessible. Watch this space.

The LiverSWAT Project

After losing my father to liver cancer in 2024, we created LiverSWAT at NishkamSWAT, fundraising for FibroScan liver screening roadshows across the UK.
